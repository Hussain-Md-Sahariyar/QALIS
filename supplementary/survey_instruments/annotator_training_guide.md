# QALIS Annotator Training Guide

**FC-4 Factual Precision & TI-2 Explanation Faithfulness Annotation Panels**

Version: 1.0 | Study: QALIS QUATIC 2025 | IRB: QUATIC-2025-IRB-Annex-B

---

## Welcome

Thank you for participating in the QALIS human annotation panel. Your judgments
contribute to the validity of two key metrics in our quality framework:

- **FC-4 Factual Precision**: Does the LLM system's output contain factually correct claims?
- **TI-2 Explanation Faithfulness**: Does the system's stated explanation faithfully reflect its output?

This guide takes approximately 45 minutes to read and includes practice items.
All annotators complete a 20-item pilot annotation before beginning live annotation.

---

## Part 1: Study Overview

### What you are annotating

You will be shown individual **LLM system outputs** — responses generated by a deployed
AI system to a user query. For each item, you will assess one specific quality criterion
(FC-4 or TI-2, depending on your panel assignment).

### Systems you will see outputs from

Four production systems participated in this study:

| Code | Domain | What it does |
|------|--------|-------------|
| S1 | Customer Support | Answers customer service queries for a retail business |
| S2 | Code Assistant | Reviews and fixes code; explains bugs to software engineers |
| S3 | Document QA | Summarises documents and answers questions from uploaded files |
| S4 | Medical Triage | Assists with clinical triage queries (high-stakes domain) |

You will see the system code (S1–S4) for each item to provide context.

### Annotation volume and timeline

- **FC-4 panel**: ~267 items per annotator (800 total ÷ 3 annotators)
- **TI-2 panel**: ~167 items per annotator (500 total ÷ 3 annotators)
- **Expected time**: 3–4 hours per annotator (spread over multiple sessions)
- **Platform**: Internal web interface (link provided separately)

## Part 2: FC-4 — Factual Precision

### What FC-4 measures

FC-4 measures whether **individual factual claims** in a system response are accurate.
It is operationalised as: *proportion of verifiable claims that are factually correct*.

This metric is especially important for S3 (document QA) and S4 (medical triage),
where factual errors can have serious consequences.

### Annotation unit

Each annotation item consists of:
1. **System code** (S1–S4)
2. **User query** — what the user asked
3. **Context** — the source document(s) the system had access to (for S3 and S4)
4. **System response** — the full LLM output
5. **Highlighted claim** — one specific factual claim extracted from the response

You will judge **the highlighted claim only** (not the response as a whole).

### Label set

| Label | When to use |
|-------|------------|
| **CORRECT** | The claim is factually accurate AND is supported by available evidence |
| **INCORRECT** | The claim is factually wrong OR directly contradicts the context/evidence |
| **UNVERIFIABLE** | The claim cannot be assessed because no relevant evidence was provided |

### Decision rules

**CORRECT** — use when:
- The claim matches the context verbatim or is a valid paraphrase
- The claim is common knowledge not requiring specific evidence (e.g. "water boils at 100°C")
- The claim is supported by the provided context chunk

**INCORRECT** — use when:
- The claim contradicts something stated in the context (hallucination)
- A number, date, name, or policy detail is wrong
- The claim is plausible but directly countered by available evidence

**UNVERIFIABLE** — use when:
- The claim makes reference to external information not present in the context
- The system was asked an open-ended question with no ground truth provided
- The claim is speculative or opinion-framed ("patients may prefer...")
- The context is ambiguous and does not clearly support or refute the claim

### Additional fields

- **Confidence (1–5)**: How confident are you in your label? (1 = very uncertain, 5 = certain)
- **Evidence snippet**: Copy-paste the relevant phrase from the context that supports your label.
  Leave blank for UNVERIFIABLE.
- **Notes** (optional): Flag anything unusual (ambiguous claim, context gap, etc.)

### Practice items — FC-4

**Item P1** (S3, Document QA)

> **Query**: What is the company's data retention policy?
>
> **Context**: "Personal data shall be retained for no longer than 3 years from the date
> of collection, unless required by law to retain longer."
>
> **Claim to judge**: "The retention period is 3 years."

*Answer*: **CORRECT** — matches context verbatim. Confidence: 5.

---

**Item P2** (S4, Medical Triage)

> **Query**: What is the first-line treatment for type 2 diabetes?
>
> **Context**: [No context provided — open-domain query]
>
> **Claim to judge**: "Metformin is typically the first-line pharmacological treatment."

*Answer*: **UNVERIFIABLE** — no context provided. (The claim is medically accurate,
but we cannot verify it from context, so it is UNVERIFIABLE by protocol.)

---

**Item P3** (S1, Customer Support)

> **Query**: How long do I have to return an item?
>
> **Context**: "All purchases can be returned within 30 days of delivery."
>
> **Claim to judge**: "You have 60 days to return your item."

*Answer*: **INCORRECT** — contradicts context (context says 30 days, claim says 60).
Confidence: 5. Evidence: "returned within 30 days of delivery."

## Part 3: TI-2 — Explanation Faithfulness

### What TI-2 measures

TI-2 measures whether the system's **stated explanation** for its output accurately
reflects what the output actually says or does. A system may produce a correct response
but give a misleading or inconsistent explanation for it.

This is particularly relevant for S4 (medical triage), where explanations support
clinical decision-making.

### Annotation unit

Each item consists of:
1. **System code** (S1–S4)
2. **User query**
3. **System response** — the full LLM output
4. **System explanation** — the system's stated rationale (may be in the same response,
   or in a separate "explanation" field produced by the system)

You will judge whether the explanation faithfully reflects the response.

### Label set

| Label | When to use |
|-------|------------|
| **FAITHFUL** | The explanation accurately describes the reasoning used in the response |
| **PARTIALLY_FAITHFUL** | The explanation is mostly correct but omits or distorts a significant step |
| **UNFAITHFUL** | The explanation contradicts or is unrelated to what the response actually does |
| **NO_EXPLANATION** | The system did not provide an explanation (mark as N/A) |

### Decision rules

**FAITHFUL** — use when:
- The explanation step-by-step traces the logic actually present in the response
- Key decisions in the response are mentioned in the explanation
- Minor phrasing differences are acceptable (not every word must match)

**PARTIALLY_FAITHFUL** — use when:
- The explanation covers the main point but skips a step that was clearly present
- The explanation introduces a rationale not supported by the response
- One of multiple reasoning chains is omitted

**UNFAITHFUL** — use when:
- The explanation describes a process not visible in the response
- The explanation attributes the response to a source not used
- The explanation contradicts the response (says "I found X" but response says Y)

### Practice items — TI-2

**Item Q1** (S3, Document QA)

> **Response**: "The contract terminates on 31 December 2025."
>
> **Explanation**: "I found the termination date by searching for the word 'expires'
> in the document."

*Answer*: **PARTIALLY_FAITHFUL** — the response gives the correct date, but the
explanation implies a keyword search for "expires" which may not have been the
mechanism. Without more information, this is partially faithful at best.

---

**Item Q2** (S4, Medical Triage)

> **Response**: "Based on the symptom profile, this presentation is consistent with
> a STEMI. Immediate cardiology referral is recommended."
>
> **Explanation**: "I identified ST-elevation indicators and compared them to STEMI
> diagnostic criteria. The recommendation follows from this classification."

*Answer*: **FAITHFUL** — the explanation matches the response's reasoning chain
(symptom classification → diagnostic criteria → recommendation).

## Part 4: Workflow and Quality Checks

### Session guidelines

- Aim for 30–50 items per annotation session to maintain concentration
- Take at least a 5-minute break between sessions
- If you are unsure about an item, use the Notes field and flag it for discussion
- Do not discuss specific items with other annotators during the annotation period

### Inter-annotator agreement checks

IAA is recomputed every 50 items. If Fleiss κ drops below 0.65, a calibration
session will be scheduled with all annotators before annotation resumes.

### Adjudication

For items with annotator disagreement (> 33% disagreement rate):
- Majority vote is used as the primary label
- Items with 3-way disagreement are flagged for senior researcher review
- You may be contacted to discuss your reasoning on flagged items

### Questions and escalation

Contact the study coordinator (details in the annotation platform) for:
- Items you cannot classify after 2+ minutes of consideration
- Items where the provided context appears to be missing or truncated
- Any concerns about the annotation process
