# Participant Consent Form — QALIS Practitioner Interview Study

**Study**: Quality Assessment Framework for LLM-Integrated Software Systems (QALIS)  
**IRB reference**: QUATIC-2025-IRB-Annex-B  
**Version**: 1.0 | **Date**: October 2024  

---

## Purpose of the Study

You are being invited to participate in a research study aimed at understanding
how practitioners evaluate the quality of large language model (LLM)-integrated
software systems in production. The findings will be used to develop and validate
a multi-dimensional quality assessment framework (QALIS) for such systems.

This study is being conducted by a team of academic researchers. The results will
be submitted to QUATIC 2025 (18th International Conference on the Quality of
Information and Communications Technology).

---

## What Participation Involves

- A single audio-recorded video call lasting approximately 50–80 minutes
- A semi-structured interview about your experiences with LLM-integrated software quality
- Optional review of preliminary findings (member checking) approximately 4–6 weeks later

---

## Confidentiality and Anonymisation

Your participation is **strictly confidential**. To protect your identity:

- Your name, organisation name, product names, and vendor names will be replaced with
  descriptive codes (e.g. "Principal ML Engineer — Financial Services") in all publications
- Audio recordings will be deleted after transcription (within 14 days of the interview)
- Verbatim transcripts will be stored securely and accessible only to the research team
- De-identified excerpts may appear in publications with your approval (see below)
- Data will be retained for 5 years following publication, then securely deleted

---

## Use of Quotes

With your permission, anonymised direct quotes from the interview may be used in
research publications and presentations. You may withdraw permission for specific
quotes during the member-checking phase.

---

## Voluntary Participation

Your participation is completely voluntary. You may:
- Decline to answer any question at any time without giving a reason
- Withdraw from the study at any time before data is published
- Request that your data be deleted (up to the point of publication)
